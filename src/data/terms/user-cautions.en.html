User Cautions

VPay never stops its efforts to protect the personal credit information of its customers. Please be aware of the items below and use our international transfer services safely.

1. Be cautious not to disclose to any 3rd party your account number, password, and other PIN's (personal identification number) that may be used to identify individuals.

2. Try not to store your banking certificate verification and other important financial information in E-mails, webhards, or file format (excel or scanned picture) in your hard disk of your PC. If it is stored this way currently, we recommend you delete it immediately.

3. Try not to use numbers that are easy to predict as your password, such as date of birth, cellphone number, address, etc. Also, we recommend you set your password different from internet portals or shopping malls, and try to change your password as often as possible.

4. Change your password regularly; if you have reasons to believe that your password has been exposed, call VPay at 070-4136-4390 as soon as possible and reset your password at the password reset page.

5. After using any electronic financial services, please log-out.

6. When using 3rd party shared electronic devices in using electronic financial services, be cautious not to expose your password, and carry your banking certificate verification in portable storage devices such as Smart Cards and USB tokens.

7. Refrain from using public computers in using electronic financial services, due to your personal information being prone to being saved. If you must, please delete all related information after use.

8. Prepare your electronic device for any security breaches by installing firewall programs provided by VPay, and regularly update the program.

9. Do not input personal information (account number, password, etc) in internet sites connected to emails or bulletins. In addition, when accessing financial services online, personally type in the URL of the website.

10. Do not input personal financial information in other websites under the request of any suspicious personnel, who may be impersonating financial institutions or the government.

11. When you encounter suspicious websites or sites that demand the input of detailed financial information, immediately contact and report to related government institutions:

1. Financial Supervisory Service 1332

2. National Police Agency – Cyber Security Department 182
